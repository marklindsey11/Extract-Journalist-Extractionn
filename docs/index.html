StoryWeb: turn stories into research data

What societal challenge does your prototype address?

The project aims to improve knowledge management in journalism. Because in addition to interested citizens, there are also many groups that consume news for professional reasons, e.g. researchers from business and science and other journalists. They read the texts primarily to understand connections that have already been uncovered.

Investigative journalism is often about networks of actors: Which companies, associations and politicians are connected to each other, which mafia structures operate across national borders or which cliques and family structures are involved influence the allocation of posts. The article as a form of presentation is a hurdle: In order to remain readable (and to minimize the link to the competition), the journalist often has to omit the background story and references to other texts and stories.

The project therefore opens up a different approach. The most important connections of an actor to other characters and events should be summarized at a glance in order to facilitate both the introduction and the in-depth analysis of individual connections. Information from relevant, structured data sources can also be integrated and provide additional details for specialists.

The project wants to extract structured actor networks from journalistic texts and analyze them. It is intended to make essential relationships between the people and organizations mentioned usable as quantitative data. In this way, complex stories can be connected as data sets for future research.

StoryWeb will systematically structure research knowledge and find connections between different stories and the work of different journalists and media houses.

How do you tackle the problem?

First of all, individual media offers should be specifically selected whose reporting is suitable for the extraction of actor networks.

The implementation is to take place as a Python program that downloads articles from media sites, then extracts the article text and metadata, and identifies relevant names through various named entity extractors.

The actors mentioned should be disambiguated on the basis of joint naming with others. Through simple analyses, the text should also provide clues as to the nature of the relationships among the identified actors. These relationships are finally classified into a category system through manual feedback. An automated classification should be trained in this way.

As part of the project, exemplary networks of actors are to be extracted for suitable research complexes, cleaned up and then published on an interactive website with detailed naming of sources.

It should be tested to enter relationships between actors with existing Wikidata items including source information in the Wikidata database.

Who is your tool aimed at?

The project generates research material and presents it on an interactive website that can be integrated into the workflows of various user groups.

Journalists who receive a systematic picture of the work of their colleagues and other media and can therefore start research faster or find connections between previously separate stories. I have access to a professional network of journalists who can use the tool and provide feedback.

There are also service providers, for example in the areas of finance, law, real estate or the art trade, who want to know whether there is negative media coverage of one of their customers.
